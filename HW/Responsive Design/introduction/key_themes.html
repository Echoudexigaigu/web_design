Five Confucian Virtues

仁

Ren — Benevolence

Humaneness and compassion toward others; the moral sentiment that motivates just action and care.

义

Yi — Righteousness

Uprightness and duty; choosing the morally right course over profit or convenience.

礼

Li — Ritual Propriety

Norms, rites, and courteous conduct that structure social harmony and political order.

智

Zhi — Wisdom

Judgment and discernment; knowing how to apply principles to changing circumstances.

信

Xin — Trustworthiness

Reliability and fidelity to one’s word; the basis of credible rule and durable alliances.
