Major Figures of Wu

The rise and longevity of the state of Wu were shaped by its founding family and brilliant military commanders. Below are three of the most influential figures in the development of Eastern Wu.

Sun Ce (孙策)

Sun Ce, often called the “Little Conqueror,” rapidly unified the Jiangdong region through bold campaigns and remarkable leadership. His charisma attracted talented followers, laying the foundation for the Wu state.

Zhou Yu (周瑜)

Zhou Yu was Wu’s preeminent strategist and commander, best known for orchestrating the decisive victory at the Battle of Red Cliffs. His naval expertise and refined diplomacy made him essential to Wu’s survival and expansion.

Sun Quan (孙权)

Sun Quan ruled Wu for over five decades, balancing military caution with political maturity. His long and stable reign transformed Wu from a regional base into a fully established kingdom.
